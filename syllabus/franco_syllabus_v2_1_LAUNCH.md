# Franco — Syllabus v2.1 — LAUNCH REALISTIC
### *Same curriculum, phased so a solo founder can actually ship it*

> **What changed from v2:** Lesson lengths cut to retention-friendly caps. Curriculum phased into 4 launch waves. Overloaded lessons split. Practice time moved from video → in-app drills. Production budget and calendar added so you don't burn out.

---

## 🎯 The Big Strategic Shift

| | v2 (Original) | v2.1 (Launch) |
|---|---|---|
| Lessons before launch | 195 (impossible) | **25 video + 170 text/AI** |
| Total video hours | 159h | **~40h video produced over 12 months** |
| First-launch budget | $19,000–38,000 HeyGen + ~600h scripting | **~$3,000 HeyGen + ~50h scripting for v1.0 launch** |
| Time to first launch | 4–6 months | **6 weeks (Foundation 25 only)** |
| Risk | Burnout, never ship | **Ship, learn, iterate** |

**The philosophy:** Ship Foundation 25 as polished video. The other 170 lessons launch as text + Sophie AI drills + cultural notes + speaking prompts — fully usable. Add one new video lesson per week post-launch. Users get a complete *learning* experience on day one and a *richer* one every week.

## ⏱ New Lesson Length Policy (THIS IS THE BIG FIX)

The old syllabus had lessons at 30–90 minutes. Nobody finishes those. New caps:

| Level | Video MAX | In-app practice | Total per lesson |
|---|---|---|---|
| Foundation | **8–10 min** | 5–8 min | 15 min |
| A1 | **8–10 min** | 6–8 min | 18 min |
| A2 | **10–12 min** | 8–10 min | 22 min |
| B1 | **12–15 min** | 10–12 min | 27 min |
| B2 | **12–15 min** | 10–12 min | 27 min |
| CLB Intensive | **15–20 min** | full mock in separate exam-sim screen | 35 min |

**Why this matters:** YouTube optimal retention window = 8–12 min. Duolingo's average lesson = 5 min. Users open the app expecting *one bite*, not a meal. Your competitor is TikTok, not university.

The "long practice" gets *moved into the app* — speaking drills, flashcards, AI conversations with Sophie. The video teaches; the app drills. Don't conflate them.

# PHASE 1 — Foundation 25 (LAUNCH BLOCKER)

**Status:** Must be 100% filmed before App Store submission of v1.1
**Theme:** *Survive your first month in Canada*
**Audience:** Brand new immigrant, zero French, week 1 in Quebec

> **Production rule for Foundation:** This is your shop window. Real Montreal b-roll wherever possible (pharmacy, IGA, café, metro, snowy street). HeyGen avatar for teaching segments, real footage for "real scene" segments.

## Unit 1 — Survival (Your first 48 hours)

### `f-01` — Bonjour Canada — The 5 Magic Words
- **Objective:** Greet and respond, without freezing
- **Key phrases:** Bonjour, Merci, S'il vous plaît, Oui/Non, Excusez-moi
- **Grammar:** None — pure phrases
- **Cultural note:** In Quebec, NOT saying bonjour when you enter a shop is rude
- **Real scene:** Walking into a Jean Coutu pharmacy
- **Length:** 9 min video + 6 min app drill = **15 min**
- **Script status:** ✅ WRITTEN (see `lesson-scripts/f-01-bonjour-canada.md`)

### `f-02` — French Pronunciation Part 1 — The Vowels You're Missing
- **Objective:** Produce 4 priority French vowels: u, ou, nasal -on/-an, é/è
- **Cultural note:** Quebecers don't expect a perfect accent. They expect EFFORT.
- **Real scene:** Saying "rue Sainte-Catherine" to a taxi driver
- **Reviews:** f-01
- **Length:** 8 min + 7 min = **15 min**
- **Note:** v2 had 8 sounds in one lesson — split. Consonants moved to a later interstitial.

### `f-03` — Je m'appelle — Introducing Yourself
- **Objective:** Tell name, origin, that you're learning French
- **Key phrases:** Je m'appelle ___, Je viens de ___, J'apprends le français, Enchanté(e)
- **Cultural note:** Saying "j'apprends" instantly builds bridges
- **Real scene:** Meeting another parent at your kid's school
- **Reviews:** f-01, f-02
- **Length:** 8 min + 6 min = **14 min**

### `f-04` — Numbers 0–20 — Hearing Prices, Times, Phones
- **Objective:** Understand and say any number 0–20 in context
- **Cultural note:** Quebec uses "soixante-dix" (not Belgian "septante")
- **Real scene:** Paying at the cash at IGA
- **Reviews:** f-01, f-03
- **Length:** 8 min + 7 min = **15 min**

### `f-05` — When You Don't Understand — The Recovery Toolkit
- **Objective:** Recover gracefully when you don't understand
- **Key phrases:** Pardon? • Je ne comprends pas • Pouvez-vous parler plus lentement? • Pouvez-vous répéter? • J'apprends.
- **Cultural note:** Quebecers SLOW DOWN when you say "j'apprends." They will NOT switch to English if you ask politely in French.
- **Real scene:** At a Service Canada appointment
- **Reviews:** f-01, f-03
- **Length:** 9 min + 6 min = **15 min**

### `f-06` — Week 1 Capstone — Your First Real Conversation
- **Objective:** String f-01 through f-05 into one 60-second exchange
- **Cultural note:** Most quit before this point. You didn't.
- **Real scene:** Buying coffee + croissant at Première Moisson
- **Reviews:** f-01, f-02, f-03, f-04, f-05
- **Length:** 9 min + 8 min = **17 min**

## Unit 2 — Identity (Who you are in French)

### `f-07` — Être — The "To Be" Verb
- **Objective:** Use être for nationality, job, status (all 6 forms)
- **Cultural note:** Quebec uses "tu" faster than France
- **Real scene:** Filling out a form at Service Québec
- **Reviews:** f-03
- **Length:** 9 min + 7 min = **16 min**

### `f-08` — Avoir — The "To Have" Verb
- **Objective:** Use avoir for age, family, hunger, appointments
- **Cultural note:** In French you HAVE hunger ("j'ai faim") — not "you ARE hungry"
- **Real scene:** Telling receptionist "j'ai un rendez-vous à 14 heures"
- **Reviews:** f-03, f-04, f-07
- **Length:** 9 min + 7 min = **16 min**

### `f-09` — Your Family
- **Objective:** Describe immediate family using avoir + être
- **Key phrases:** mon mari/ma femme • mon fils/ma fille • mes parents • mon frère/ma sœur
- **Real scene:** Coffee with a new neighbor who asks about your family
- **Reviews:** f-03, f-07, f-08
- **Length:** 8 min + 6 min = **14 min**

### `f-10` — Where You Live
- **Objective:** Say where you live, with whom, in what kind of home
- **Cultural note:** Quebec addresses include the borough ("à Verdun", "à Rosemont")
- **Real scene:** Telling a delivery person your address over the phone
- **Reviews:** f-04, f-07
- **Length:** 7 min + 6 min = **13 min**

### `f-11` — Your Work — What You Do
- **Objective:** State profession, previous profession, what you study
- **Cultural note:** Immigrants often arrive overqualified. Honesty about your background builds trust.
- **Real scene:** Networking event at a francisation centre
- **Reviews:** f-03, f-07
- **Length:** 8 min + 6 min = **14 min**

### `f-12` — Week 2 Capstone — Tell Your Story
- **Objective:** Deliver a 90-second self-introduction
- **Real scene:** First day at francisation class
- **Reviews:** f-07, f-08, f-09, f-10, f-11
- **Length:** 9 min + 8 min = **17 min**

## Unit 3 — Numbers in Real Life (Time, Money, Quantities)

### `f-13` — Numbers 20–1000 — Prices & Dates
- **Objective:** Understand numbers up to 1000 (rent, salary, year, prices)
- **Cultural note:** Quebec rent is in "piastres" — "1200 piastres par mois"
- **Real scene:** Apartment hunting — landlord says monthly rent
- **Reviews:** f-04
- **Length:** 9 min + 7 min = **16 min**

### `f-14` — Telling Time (12h & 24h)
- **Objective:** Ask and tell time in both formats
- **Cultural note:** Quebec uses 24h for appointments. "14h30" = 2:30 PM.
- **Real scene:** Booking a medical appointment over the phone
- **Reviews:** f-04, f-13
- **Length:** 8 min + 7 min = **15 min**

### `f-15` — Days, Months, Dates
- **Objective:** Say any date, write dates on Canadian forms
- **Cultural note:** Canadian forms = DD/MM/YYYY. #1 immigrant form mistake.
- **Real scene:** Filling out a health card application
- **Reviews:** f-13, f-14
- **Length:** 7 min + 6 min = **13 min**

### `f-16` — Money & Paying at the Cash
- **Objective:** Complete a purchase confidently
- **Cultural note:** Quebec taxes added at checkout (TPS 5% + TVQ 9.975%) — sticker price is NEVER what you pay
- **Real scene:** Paying at any Quebec cashier
- **Reviews:** f-04, f-13
- **Length:** 8 min + 6 min = **14 min**

### `f-17` — Weather & Quebec Winter
- **Objective:** Talk about weather, understand warnings
- **Cultural note:** "Froid de canard" = duck-cold (very cold). Locals love this expression.
- **Real scene:** Small talk in winter — Quebec's #1 conversation starter
- **Reviews:** f-13
- **Length:** 7 min + 6 min = **13 min**

### `f-18` — Week 3 Capstone — Book Your First Appointment
- **Objective:** Make a phone appointment in French (doctor, hairdresser, daycare)
- **Cultural note:** Booking in French unlocks ALL Quebec services
- **Real scene:** Calling a CLSC to book a flu shot
- **Reviews:** f-13, f-14, f-15, f-16
- **Length:** 9 min + 8 min = **17 min**

- **Objective:** Buy medication, get pharmacist advice

### `f-24` — Directions & The Metro

# 📱 App Implementation — What Changes in the Code

To support this phased launch, the app needs these updates in `src/FrancoApp.jsx`:

```js
// New lesson schema field:
const LESSON_F01 = {
  id: "f-01",
  title: "Bonjour Canada — The 5 Magic Words",
  level: "Foundation",
  unit: 1,
  videoStatus: "filmed",        // "filmed" | "scripted" | "text-only"
  videoUrl: "https://youtube.com/...",
  videoDurationMin: 9,
  practiceDurationMin: 6,
  premium: false,                // Foundation always free
  // ... existing fields
};

// When videoStatus !== "filmed", show:
// "📚 Practice this lesson with Sophie now —
//  🎬 Premium video coming MM/YYYY"
```

**Premium gating:**
- **Free tier:** Foundation 25 (full video + text + Sophie)
- **Premium tier:** A1 + A2 + B1 + B2 + CLB Intensive (text + Sophie + video as it drops)

This means **users can start completely free** with a real, high-quality 25-lesson course — which is more than most paid apps give away. They convert when they hit the A1 paywall, motivated by sunk-cost progress.
