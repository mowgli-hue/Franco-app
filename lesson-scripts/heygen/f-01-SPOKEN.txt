Imagine this. You just landed in Montreal. It's your first morning. You walk into a small shop to buy water, and the owner looks up at you and says one word. Bonjour.

You freeze. You don't know what to say back. You smile awkwardly, you grab the water, you pay, and you leave… feeling a little bit invisible.

Today, that ends. By the end of this lesson, you will know the five magic words that unlock almost every door in Quebec. Five words. That's it. And I promise you, the moment you start using them, people will treat you completely differently.

So here's what you'll walk away with today. You'll greet people the way Canadians actually do it. You'll say thank you, please, yes, no, and excuse me, naturally. And most importantly, you'll know when to use each one, because in Quebec, the timing matters as much as the word. Ready? Allons-y. Let's go.

Okay. Magic word number one. The one you'll use every single day in Canada. Bonjour. It means "hello," and also "good morning," and also "good day." One word, three jobs. Repeat after me… Bonjour.

Beautiful. Now, here's something you must know. In Quebec, when you walk into a shop, a bakery, a pharmacy, even a doctor's office, you say bonjour to the person behind the counter. Not saying it is considered a little bit cold. So this tiny word is the ticket that says, "I respect you, I see you."

Magic word number two. Merci. That's your "thank you." Use it for everything. Someone holds the door, merci. The cashier hands you change, merci. Repeat after me… Merci. And a little upgrade: to say "thank you very much," you say Merci beaucoup.

Magic word number three. This one's a little longer, so we'll take it slow. S'il vous plaît. It means "please." Repeat with me… S'il vous plaît. Use it when you ask for anything. "Un café, s'il vous plaît." A coffee, please. Without it, you sound like you're giving an order. With it, you sound like a local.

Magic words four and five, and they come as a pair. Oui. Yes. Non. No. Listen carefully: oui is softer than the English "we." Oui… Non…

And the bonus, magic word number six, because I want you to win. Excusez-moi. "Excuse me." You'll use it when you bump into someone on the metro, or need to interrupt politely. Repeat… Excusez-moi.

That's it. That's your toolkit. Bonjour. Merci. S'il vous plaît. Oui. Non. Excusez-moi. Six words. You can survive your first week in Canada with just these.

Let me show you what this looks like in real life. I'm walking into a Jean Coutu pharmacy in Montreal to buy a bottle of water. The cashier says, "Bonjour!" I say, "Bonjour!" I put my water on the counter, and I say, "Une bouteille d'eau, s'il vous plaît." A bottle of water, please. She tells me the price, I pay, and I say, "Merci!" She says, "Bonne journée!" Have a good day. And I answer, "Vous aussi!" You too. The whole thing takes twenty seconds. You just bought water, in French, in Canada. Welcome.

Now it's your turn. Say these words out loud with me. Don't whisper them, say them. Your mouth needs to learn the shape. Bonjour… Merci… S'il vous plaît… Oui… Non… Excusez-moi… Beautiful work. If you stumbled on s'il vous plaît, that's completely normal. Everybody does. Say it ten more times today, and by tomorrow it'll feel natural.

Here's your mission, and I really want you to do this. Today, before you go to bed, use bonjour one time, in real life, with a real person. The cashier, the barista, your neighbour, your Uber driver. Just one bonjour. I promise, they will smile, they will respond, and something inside you will shift, because you'll realize, "I just spoke French, in Canada, and it worked."

Quick recap. Here are your six magic words one more time. Bonjour, hello. Merci, thank you. S'il vous plaît, please. Oui, yes. Non, no. Excusez-moi, excuse me. Six words. You officially have a French toolkit. You belong here now.
