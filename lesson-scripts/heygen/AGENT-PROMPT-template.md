## MASTER CREATIVE BRIEF (same for every lesson)

You are producing an episode of "French with Sophie," a warm, friendly French lessons series
for newcomers and immigrants living in Canada (CLB / TEF Canada audience).

NON-NEGOTIABLES:
- Host / avatar: "franco ki di" (Sophie), a warm French-Canadian teacher. Keep her on screen for all
  teaching moments, seated with the lesson whiteboard behind her.
- Format: 16:9 landscape, 1080p. Add burned-in captions/subtitles for the whole video.
- Voice: Cassidy (multilingual). Keep French words pronounced clearly.
- Tone: warm, encouraging, never rushed — a real teacher who gives the learner time to repeat and write.

B-ROLL RULES (this is what makes it feel produced):
- Cut to relevant B-roll during the HOOK and the real-life MODEL scenes; return to Sophie + whiteboard
  for the actual teaching.
- Use CANADIAN / QUEBEC b-roll only: Montreal streets, the metro, a snowy street, a pharmacy
  (Jean Coutu / Pharmaprix), a café or dépanneur, a grocery checkout, a government service counter.
  No Eiffel Tower, no Paris — this is CANADA.
- Keep cuts gentle and slow; this is a calm teaching video, not a fast ad.

ON-SCREEN TEXT RULES:
- Every time Sophie teaches a French word, show it BIG on screen with its pronunciation and meaning,
  e.g.  Bonjour  [bawn-ZHOOR]  —  hello.  Spell French EXACTLY as given (keep accents é è à ç).
- During a "write this down" moment, show a clean card: "✍️ Write this down" + the words.
- Open with a title card: the lesson number + title + "French with Sophie." Close on "À demain! 🍁".

MUSIC: soft, warm, low instrumental under the whole video.
